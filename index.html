How To Use It

How to use this site to find overseas buyers for Japan-sourced gacha

1

Choose the first overseas markets

Use the buyer pipeline and score columns to decide which markets to contact first. Prioritize shops that already sell anime goods, blind boxes, capsule toys, figures, arcade prizes, or Japanese character merchandise.

2

Prepare a buyer contact list

For each market, collect shop name, owner or buyer name, Instagram, email, website form, phone, country, and what they already sell. The goal is to contact decision makers, not general customer-service inboxes only.

3

Offer a low-risk test lot

Do not start by asking for a large order. Offer 80-160 capsules across 3-5 current Japanese series, with real photos before shipment, invoice, packing list, shipping options, and a clear landed-cost estimate.

4

Show why buying from Japan helps them

Explain that a Japan-side source can check product condition, group multiple series, send current photos, ship quickly, and find items that are harder to source locally. Make reliability the main selling point.

5

Use the website as a sales presentation

Send this page when contacting overseas stores. It explains the market logic, buyer types, test-lot system, Japan-side sourcing method, product photo process, invoice, packing list, and repeat-order workflow.

6

Scale by market response

Track who replies, which countries ask for prices, which series they request, and what shipping concerns they have. Use those replies to decide which overseas market is worth deeper sourcing and repeated outreach.

Practical first move

Prepare a simple lead spreadsheet with: country, shop name, buyer name, email, Instagram, product fit, first message date, reply status, requested products, sample-lot size, shipping estimate, and next follow-up date.
